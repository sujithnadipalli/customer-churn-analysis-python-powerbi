## 📌 Project Overview

Customer churn is a critical business challenge for banks and financial institutions. Understanding why customers leave helps organizations improve retention strategies, increase profitability, and enhance customer satisfaction.

This project combines **Python-based Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)** and **Power BI Dashboarding** to identify customer churn patterns and generate actionable business insights.

---

## 🎯 Business Objective

The primary objective of this project is to:

* Analyze customer churn behavior
* Identify high-risk customer segments
* Discover factors influencing customer attrition
* Generate actionable business recommendations
* Build interactive dashboards for decision-making

## 📌 Key Insights

### Customer Churn Overview

* Total Customers: 10,000
* Churned Customers: 2,037
* Churn Rate: 20.37%

### Geographic Insights

* France contributes the highest share of customer churn.
* Germany and Spain show comparatively lower churn volumes.

### Age-Based Insights

* Customers aged 51+ exhibit the highest churn rate.
* Customers aged 18–30 show the lowest churn tendency.

### Customer Activity Insights

* Inactive customers are significantly more likely to churn.
* Active engagement strongly improves retention.

### Product Usage Insights

* Customers with only one product account for the majority of churn.
* Multi-product customers show better retention behavior.

### Financial Insights

* High-balance customers contribute a large portion of churned accounts.
* Credit score variations influence customer retention patterns.

### Customer Relationship Insights

* Long-tenure customers represent a notable share of churned customers.
* Customer loyalty alone does not guarantee retention.
